Project Overview
This project analyzes a retail sales dataset using SQL Server to uncover business insights related to revenue performance, customer behavior, product sales, and geographical trends.

The objective was to apply SQL querying techniques to answer real-world business questions and transform raw sales data into actionable recommendations.

⸻

Business Problem

Organizations generate large volumes of sales data daily, but data alone does not create value. Businesses need analytical insights to identify high-performing products, understand customer purchasing behavior, monitor revenue trends, and make informed strategic decisions.

This project addresses these challenges by using SQL to analyze sales performance and generate actionable business recommendations.

Key Findings

Revenue Performance

* Total Revenue Generated: $10,032,628.85
* Best Performing Year: 2004 ($4.72M)
* Best Performing Month: November ($2.12M)

Insights: 

Revenue peaked in 2004 and November, suggesting strong seasonal and yearly sales patterns.

⸻

Customer Analysis

Top Customer:

* Euro Shopping Channel ($912,294.11)

Top 10 customers contributed approximately 29.45% of total revenue.

Insight

A relatively small group of customers drives a significant portion of sales, indicating customer concentration risk.

⸻

Product Analysis

Top Selling Product:

* S18_3232 (1,774 units sold)

Highest Revenue Product Line:

* Classic Cars ($3.92M)

Insight

Classic Cars is the company’s strongest product category and primary revenue driver.

⸻

Regional Analysis

Top Country:

* USA ($3.63M)

Top Territory:

* EMEA ($4.98M)

Insight

The USA and EMEA region represent the company’s most valuable markets.

⸻

Business Recommendations

1. Strengthen customer retention programs for high-value customers.
2. Increase inventory and marketing efforts before November to capitalize on peak demand.
3. Invest further in the Classic Cars product line.
4. Expand successful sales strategies from the USA and EMEA to other markets.
5. Diversify the customer base to reduce revenue concentration risk.
6. Maintain sufficient stock levels for top-selling products.
